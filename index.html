Alexander McQueen

Designer Extraordinaire

Behind the Brand

Lee Alexander McQueen was born on March 17, 1969 in Lewisham, London to a working class family. His father was a taxi driver while his mother was a teacher.

McQueen had five siblings, with him being the youngest. He was close to his three sisters and his aunt. He was a self-described "pink sheep" of the family - a misfit but not a reject.
A young Alexander McQueen

"I always wanted to be a designer. I read books on fashion from the age of 12."

McQueen, who was called "Lee" by his friends, had already expressed early on that he had wanted to be a fashion designer. He dropped out of school when he was 16 to train at Savile Row at Gieves & Hawkes, a bespoke men's tailor and menswear line. There, he reportedly once embroidered a suit for the Prince of Wales with the words "I am a cunt" in the lining.

McQueen was also an avid scuba diver and had used this passion of his as an inspiration for some of his works, most notably for his Spring/Summer collection for 2010.

McQueen was openly gay and was already aware of his sexuality at the age of six. He once expressed that, "I was sure of myself and my sexuality and I've got nothing to hide. I went straight from my mother's womb onto the gay parade." He, however, only came out to his family when he was eighteen, and after an initially rough period, they were able to accept him completely.

In 2000, he married his partner George Forsyth in an unofficial ceremony in Ibiza as same-sex marriage was not yet legal in Spain at that time. However, the marriage lasted only for a year.

Wild Fashion

McQueen was known for his extravagant, out of the ordinary, incredibly shocking, often controversial and thought provoking designs. Likewise, these designs were showcased in equally lavish and unconventional runway shows.
He was also not one to shy away from using technology and new innovations in his creations and runway shows unlike most of the other high profile designers who stuck to more traditional designs and shows.

"Fashion should be a form of escapism, and not a form of imprisonment."

Savage Beauty

All throughout his career, McQueen had created a lot of iconic and unforgettable designs. These designs were showcased in an art exhibition as a form of tribute for the designer by the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City last 2011. The exhibit was aptly entitled "Savage Beauty".
